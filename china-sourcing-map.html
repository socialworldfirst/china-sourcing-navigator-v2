The China Sourcing Map

A free 328-page guide to sourcing from China — city by city, step by step.

China is big. You don't need to learn all of it.

If you're buying electronics, you probably need Shenzhen. If you're buying leather bags, you probably need Guangzhou. If you're buying furniture, Foshan is 30 minutes away.

The hard part is knowing where to look. Most people start on Alibaba and contact dozens of suppliers hoping one works out. That takes time and costs money.

This guide maps out which cities make what, where the factories are, and how to tell a manufacturer from a middleman. We put it together because we move money to Chinese suppliers every day and kept seeing the same mistakes.
