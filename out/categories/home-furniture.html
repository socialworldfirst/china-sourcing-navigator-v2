Furniture Manufacturing in China

China produces roughly a third of all furniture manufactured on Earth and exports more than US$36 billion worth every year — four times the value of second-place Germany. Guangdong Province is the centre of gravity: Foshan's Shunde district alone houses 7,000+ furniture factories and 20,000+ trading companies, with annual output exceeding US$14 billion.

The supply chain runs from Longjiang's 1,200 major factories (where the first modern sofa in China was produced in 1977) through Lecong's 4 million square metres of showroom space and out to global markets via Nansha port. Dongguan handles the export-grade office and flat-pack side. Yiwu covers home accessories at the lowest MOQ in China. For buyers wanting anything from a single sample chair to 10,000 hotel room sets, the infrastructure exists to deliver it.

World’s largest furniture wholesale market — 4 million sqm of showroom space, 7,000+ factories

Foshan’s Shunde district is the undisputed centre of global furniture manufacturing and trade. The Lecong–Longjiang corridor runs 10 km along Provincial Road S121: Lecong is the “front shop” with 4 million square metres of showroom space across 180+ complexes, 3,450+ dealers, and 300,000+ product types. Longjiang is the “back factory” — 1,200+ major factories, 7,000+ manufacturing enterprises, and 26,000+ proprietary brands producing sofas, dining sets, beds, office furniture, outdoor furniture, and hotel fit-outs.

Key venues include Louvre International Furniture Expo Center (380,000 sqm, 2,000+ brands, 2 million visitors a year), Red Star Macalline Lecong (the only Red Star branch operating as a wholesale expo — 90%+ factory-direct), and Sunlink Furniture City (200,000 sqm across 13 buildings). Nearby Jiujiang town produces 1 in every 10 mattresses sold worldwide. CIFF Guangzhou, the world’s largest furniture trade fair (850,000 sqm, 4,900+ exhibitors), runs every March at the Canton Fair Complex — 45 minutes from Lecong.

Logistics gateway — CIFF host city, Canton Fair Phase 2, and Nansha port

Guangzhou is not where furniture is manufactured — Foshan is. But at 20 km apart and connected by metro, the two cities function as a single sourcing cluster. Buyers fly into Baiyun International Airport, visit Lecong showrooms, attend CIFF (March) or Canton Fair Phase 2 (April/October), and ship from Nansha port. All without leaving the Pearl River Delta.

Canton Fair Phase 2 covers consumer goods, home products, furniture, kitchenware, and decorative gifts — over 200,000 buyers across 130,000+ sqm. Guangzhou also has a home accessories wholesale cluster around Yide Road and in Panyu, plus furniture logistics parks that consolidate shipments for international freight forwarding. For anyone buying furniture in Foshan, Guangzhou is the practical base.

China’s export-grade furniture hub — office, flat-pack, and premium upholstered

Dongguan has carved a distinct niche from Foshan: export-oriented, compliance-ready, premium. The city’s 2,000+ furniture manufacturers serve North American and European retailers directly, with factories geared for fire-retardant standards, sustainability requirements, CARB Phase 2 formaldehyde limits, and packaging regulations from the outset. The Houjie–Dalingshan corridor is the core — Houjie handles trade shows and exhibitions, Dalingshan handles manufacturing and export.

Dongguan’s speciality is office furniture, flat-pack/RTA (ready-to-assemble) furniture, and high-end upholstered products. Major international brands source their contract furniture here. If Foshan is the place to walk showrooms and compare thousands of styles, Dongguan is the place to commission a factory run of 5,000 identical desks built to EN or ANSI/BIFMA spec.

Home accessories capital — 3,000+ shops, lowest MOQ entry point in China

Yiwu International Trade City, District 1, Floor 3 is dedicated to home decor, covering everything from ceramic arts and crystal crafts to photo frames, candles, artificial flowers, and wall hangings. Over 3,000 shops carry Christmas decorations, wedding supplies, and household accessories with MOQs as low as one carton. For Amazon and Shopify sellers wanting home decor variety at minimal commitment, Yiwu is the default starting point.

Yiwu does not compete with Foshan on furniture — you won’t find sofa factories here. Its strength is the accessories and small home goods layer: cushion covers, table runners, storage baskets, decorative lighting, bathroom accessories, and kitchen gadgets. The surrounding factory belt (Jinhua, Pujiang, Dongyang) produces what Yiwu sells. Dongyang, 1.5 hours south, is China’s wood carving capital with a centuries-old furniture tradition.

Furniture China expo — 3,500+ exhibitors, 350,000 sqm, design-led sourcing

Shanghai is not a furniture manufacturing city, but it hosts Furniture China (September, SNIEC Pudong) — one of the world’s top furniture trade fairs with 3,500+ exhibitors across 350,000+ sqm. CIFF Shanghai (also September) adds a second exhibition focused on office, commercial, and hospitality furniture. For buyers who cannot attend CIFF Guangzhou in March, Shanghai’s September shows are the alternative.

Shanghai’s sourcing role is as a professional services hub: sourcing agents, quality inspection firms, legal counsel for IP protection, and trade finance. Several Foshan and Dongguan factories maintain Shanghai sales offices or showrooms for international buyers who prefer to do business in a Tier 1 city.

How to Source Furniture from China

Four ways to find furniture suppliers. Shipping is the number one cost driver — factor freight into every quote.

Search on 1688

1688 is Alibaba's domestic B2B marketplace — where Chinese factories trade with each other. For furniture, that means factory-gate pricing on sofas, dining sets, beds, office desks, and home decor that Alibaba.com marks up significantly.

How to find furniture suppliers on 1688

1
Search in Chinese

Use Chinese product terms like 家具批发 (furniture wholesale), 佛山家具 (Foshan furniture), or 沙发工厂 (sofa factory).

2
Filter by furniture hubs

Set location to 佛山 (Foshan), 东莞 (Dongguan), or 广州 (Guangzhou). These three cities cover 80%+ of furniture sourcing.

3
Check factory badges

Look for 超级工厂 (Super Factory), 实力商家 (Power Merchant), and the 厂 tag (factory, not trader). Repeat buyer rate (回头率) above 20% signals reliable quality.

4
Request production samples

Never buy from showroom samples alone. Order from an actual production run and verify material spec, finish quality, and joinery before committing to bulk.
