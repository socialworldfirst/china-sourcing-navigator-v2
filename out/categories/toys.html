Toy Manufacturing in China

China produces roughly three out of every four toys sold worldwide. In 2023, traditional toy exports reached US$40.57 billion, with total toy, game, and sports equipment exports hitting $82.4 billion. Guangdong Province accounts for approximately 70% of China's toy output, powered by 5,000+ sizable manufacturers and 1.5 million workers.

The supply chain runs from Chenghai's 12,000 plastic toy factories through Guangzhou's Yide Road wholesale district and out to global markets via Shenzhen, Ningbo, and Hong Kong. Dongguan handles the electronic and brand-OEM side — Hasbro and Mattel both source from factories there. Yiwu offers the lowest entry point: 450,000+ styles, MOQs as low as 48 units, and mixed-container loading.

1 in 3 toys sold worldwide is made in Dongguan

Dongguan sits at the centre of China’s toy manufacturing triangle, alongside Shenzhen (electronics components) and Guangzhou (wholesale distribution). The city has 4,000+ toy-production enterprises and 1,500+ upstream suppliers, making it the go-to base for electronic toys, smart toys, and brand-quality OEM. Hasbro and Mattel both source from factories in Dongguan.

The key districts are Humen (major factory concentration, including Better Toys at 6,000 sqm and Weijun Toys at 8,500 sqm), Chang’an (plastic injection moulding and electronics integration for smart toys), and Zhangmutou (Jinhe and Feida Industrial Zones). For any buyer wanting app-controlled RC cars, sound-and-light toys, or designer collectibles, Dongguan’s intersection of toy design and electronics manufacturing is unmatched. International compliance standards are stronger here than in Shantou.

450,000+ toy styles under one roof — the world’s largest toy wholesale market

Yiwu International Trade City, District 1, Floor 1 is the starting point for any small-to-medium toy buyer. Over 2,000 toy suppliers occupy one floor, offering 450,000+ styles across every category: plush, RC, educational, inflatable, building blocks, and novelty. MOQs run as low as 48–200 units with order minimums from around $200.

The market operates 9am–5pm, seven days a week, 365 days a year (except a 15-day Spring Festival closure). The surrounding factory belt — Jinhua, Dongyang, Pujiang — produces what Yiwu sells. Yunhe County, 2.5 hours southwest, supplies 40% of the world’s wooden toys. Blind box and trading card segments are growing fast. For Amazon and Shopify sellers wanting variety at low cost with mixed-container loading, Yiwu is the default.

Yide Road: 1,200+ shops across China’s largest toy wholesale district

Guangzhou’s Yide Road is the distribution hub that connects Chenghai’s 12,000 factories to global buyers. The Yide Road Toys & Gifts Wholesale Market covers 43 industries, 1,900 categories, and 1.7 million product types. OneLink International Plaza (Wanling, 万菱广场) adds another layer: a 37-storey building where floors 1–7 house 1,000+ factory showrooms with 1,000,000+ SKUs, five minutes from Haizhu Square metro.

Canton Fair Phase 3 (May and November) includes a major toys section, giving Guangzhou a trade-show dimension that Yiwu and Dongguan lack. For buyers who want to compare across every toy category in a single trip — plush, plastic, electronic, educational, promotional — Guangzhou’s Yide Road district is the most efficient starting point in the world.

Asia’s largest toy trade fair — 2,000+ exhibitors from 52 countries

Hong Kong is not where toys are made, but it is where international deals happen. The HKTDC Hong Kong Toys & Games Fair (January) is Asia’s largest toy industry event and the second largest in the world, drawing 2,000+ exhibitors across 52 countries. Trends emerge here six months before retail.

Many Guangdong toy factories maintain Hong Kong trading arms for international banking, IP protection, and compliance services. For brand licensing, quality inspection coordination, and connecting with vetted Chinese manufacturers in a single event, Hong Kong is the professional front door to China’s toy industry.

China’s premium plush toy export hub — trusted by EU and North American buyers

For any buyer sourcing plush toys for Western markets, Qingdao is arguably the best single city in China. The cluster is quality-focused and compliance-ready, with factories geared toward EN71 and ASTM F963 certification from the outset. European and North American brands trust Qingdao for licensed character plush, stuffed animals, and mid-to-high-end soft toys.

Qingdao’s port infrastructure (one of China’s top 5 by container throughput) means direct shipping with shorter inland logistics than Guangdong alternatives. The city loses one star only because it is narrow — plush only, not all toy categories. But for plush-only sourcing trips, Qingdao offers a combination of quality, compliance culture, and export efficiency that Guangzhou and Dongguan cannot match in this specific niche.

How to Source Toys from China

Four ways to find toy suppliers. Safety compliance is the number one concern — verify before you commit.

Search on 1688

1688 is Alibaba's domestic B2B marketplace — where Chinese factories trade with each other. For toys, that means factory-gate pricing on plastic, plush, electronic, and educational toys that Alibaba.com marks up significantly.

How to find toy suppliers on 1688

1
Search in Chinese

Use Chinese product terms like 玩具批发 (toy wholesale), 益智玩具 (educational toys), or 毛绒玩具 (plush toys).

2
Filter by toy hubs

Set location to 东莞 (Dongguan), 广州 (Guangzhou), or 义乌 (Yiwu). These three cities cover 80%+ of toy sourcing.

3
Check factory badges

Look for 超级工厂 (Super Factory), 实力商家 (Power Merchant), and the 厂 tag (factory, not trader). Repeat buyer rate (回头率) above 20% is a good sign.

4
Verify + safety test

Cross-check the business registration. Before production, get EN71 or ASTM F963 testing at a CPSC-accepted lab. This is not optional — it is the law.
