Footwear manufacturing in China

China produced 13 billion pairs of shoes in 2024 — over 54% of the world's total. Exports reached 9.2 billion pairs valued at US$46.9 billion, giving China a 62% share of global footwear trade by volume. The industry spans three major production belts: Fujian (sneakers and sportswear), Guangdong (mid-to-high-end export shoes), and Zhejiang (leather shoes, sandals, and children's footwear).

What makes China different from Vietnam or Indonesia is not just scale — it is supply chain density. In Jinjiang, every component needed to build a finished shoe sits within a 30-minute drive. Raw materials, moulds, soles, uppers, stitching, and packaging all cluster around the same towns. That proximity compresses lead times, cuts transport costs, and makes rapid prototyping possible in ways that dispersed supply chains cannot match.

One in five pairs of sneakers on the planet is stitched together in Chendai town

Jinjiang’s Chendai town is where China’s sneaker supply chain begins. Across 38 square kilometres, 7,000+ shoe-related enterprises turn out over one billion pairs of sneakers a year, with an output value exceeding 50 billion yuan. Anta (62.4 billion RMB revenue), Xtep, Peak, 361 Degrees, and Hongxing Erke were all born here and still produce here. The local saying — ‘you can gather every component for a shoe within 30 minutes’ — captures the density: leather, textiles, synthetics, foams, rubber soles, hardware, moulds, and machinery all cluster within a 50 km radius.

Jinjiang International Shoe and Textile City is the anchor market — 1,014 merchants, 49.5 billion yuan in transaction volume (2024), with 60% of trade going overseas. The 27th Jinjiang Footwear Expo (April 2026) fills 60,000 sqm with 2,500 booths and 150,000+ visitors. For buyers who want sneakers, athletic shoes, EVA sandals, or children’s shoes at factory-gate prices, there is no closer-to-source location on earth.

China’s largest export shoe assembly base — 4,000 companies in Houjie town alone

Houjie town built its footwear cluster over 30 years ago by attracting Taiwanese and Hong Kong shoe companies looking for lower costs than their home markets. Today, roughly 4,000 companies in Houjie produce and sell leather, shoe materials, shoe machines, hardware, and accessories — of which 300 are machine makers and 800 are traders. Haopan Shoe Plaza on Houjie Road is the main wholesale centre for finished shoes and materials.

Dongguan’s shoe factories skew towards mid-to-high-end export products. International brands historically placed contract manufacturing here because the city’s quality control infrastructure, port access (Dongguan Port in Shatian), and proximity to Hong Kong made logistics predictable. Typical MOQs are 300–1,000 pairs per style. Many factories here now refuse orders under US$20 per pair as they move upmarket, so Dongguan suits buyers targeting premium positioning rather than budget volume.

Zhanxi Road — 16 markets, 4,000 merchants, 30,000 visitors a day, all within a 2 km strip

Guangzhou’s footwear trade concentrates along Zhanxi Road, a 2-kilometre wholesale strip near Guangzhou Railway Station. Sixteen specialised shoe markets — including Jinma Shoe City, Euro Commercial Plaza, and BuYunTianDi International Trade Centre — house over 4,000 merchants and draw 30,000+ daily visitors. Around 70% of Guangzhou’s leather shoe exports pass through this corridor. The product range covers men’s and women’s leather shoes, casual shoes, work boots, sneakers, and shoe materials.

Guangzhou is not where shoes are made — it is where shoes are traded. Factories in Dongguan, Jinjiang, and Wenzhou send stock here for wholesale distribution. For importers who want variety in a single trip, Zhanxi offers the broadest selection of any shoe market in China. Canton Fair Phase 3 (May and November) adds another layer, with dedicated shoe exhibition halls covering fashion shoes, sports shoes, children’s shoes, and sandals.

District 4, 3rd floor — the shoe section of the world’s largest commodity market

Yiwu International Trade City’s shoe section sits on the 3rd floor of District 4, open 365 days a year. The market serves a different buyer profile than Jinjiang or Dongguan: it is geared towards small-to-medium importers buying mixed containers of finished footwear at the lowest possible price point. Slippers, sandals, casual sneakers, and basic leather shoes dominate. MOQs can drop as low as 20–50 pairs per style for stock items.

Yiwu’s strength is logistics, not manufacturing. The city’s freight network ships to 230+ countries, and consolidation services make it easy to combine shoes with other small commodities in one container. Many Quanzhou factories maintain showrooms in Yiwu specifically to reach buyers who cannot travel to Fujian. The phrase ‘Quanzhou made, Yiwu sold’ captures the pipeline.

Trade shows, compliance, and the world’s busiest container port

Shanghai is not a footwear manufacturing city, but it is where the industry’s trade infrastructure sits. The All China Leather Exhibition (ACLE) draws 800+ exhibitors from 36 countries and 23,000 buyers every September at SNIEC. The city also hosts footwear sessions within the broader fashion week calendar. For importers focused on leather goods, materials, and machinery, Shanghai’s trade fairs are the most international in China.

Yangshan Deep Water Port handles more container volume than any port in the world. Shoes manufactured in Jinjiang, Dongguan, or Wenzhou frequently ship through Shanghai when heading to the US East Coast or Europe. Shanghai also has the deepest pool of third-party testing labs (SGS, Bureau Veritas, Intertek) for footwear compliance testing — essential for US CPSIA, EU REACH, and Australian care labelling requirements.

How to source footwear from China

Four ways to find footwear suppliers. Most successful importers use multiple approaches at different stages.

Search on 1688

1688 is Alibaba's domestic B2B marketplace — where Chinese factories trade with each other. For footwear, that means factory-gate pricing on sneakers, leather shoes, sandals, and shoe materials that Alibaba.com doesn't surface. Filter by \u664B\u6C5F (Jinjiang) for sports shoes or \u6E29\u5DDE (Wenzhou) for leather shoes.

How to find footwear suppliers on 1688

1
Search in Chinese

Use Chinese product terms like 运动鞋批发 (sports shoe wholesale), 男鞋厂家 (men’s shoe factory), 凉鞋批发 (sandal wholesale), or 童鞋厂家 (children’s shoe factory).

2
Filter by footwear hubs

Set location to 晋江 (Jinjiang), 东莞 (Dongguan), 温州 (Wenzhou), or 温岭 (Wenling). Jinjiang returns the most sports shoe results; Wenzhou for leather.

3
Check factory badges

Look for 超级工厂 (Super Factory) and 实力商家 (Power Merchant). Check repeat buyer rate (回头率) — 20%+ signals consistent quality.

4
Verify before paying

Cross-check the business registration on Tianyancha. Use WorldFirst to pay in RMB directly to the supplier’s 1688/Alipay account.
