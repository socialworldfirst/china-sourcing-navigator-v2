Lighting manufacturing in China

China exported US$56.1 billion worth of lighting products in 2024, with LED accounting for 88% of the total. One town dominates the industry: Guzhen in Zhongshan, where 45,000+ enterprises produce 70% of China's and roughly half the world's lighting. The supply chain is completely vertical — LED chips, drivers, housings, glass, acrylic, and finished fixtures are all manufactured within a few kilometres of each other.

The industry is not monolithic. Guzhen handles decorative and residential lighting. Foshan's Nanhai district produces commercial LED panels and downlights. Shenzhen leads in smart lighting — WiFi, Bluetooth, and Zigbee-connected systems with IoT integration. Knowing which cluster handles which product type is the difference between paying factory price and paying through two middlemen.

Guzhen town alone produces 70% of China’s lighting — 45,000 enterprises within a single township

Guzhen town in Zhongshan City is the undisputed lighting capital of China and one of the world’s four major professional lighting markets, alongside Italy, France, and Taiwan. Over 45,000 lighting and supporting enterprises are concentrated here, forming an industrial cluster with annual output exceeding 100 billion yuan. Products are exported to 130+ countries. The supply chain is completely vertical: LED chips, drivers, wire and cable, glass, acrylic, metal housings, and finished fixtures are all manufactured within a few kilometres of each other.

Factory-direct prices in Guzhen run 10–20% of Western retail for the same category of product, and 30–50% below Guangzhou wholesale. The product range covers everything from crystal chandeliers and decorative pendants to commercial track lighting, outdoor street lights, LED strip lighting, and smart systems. Together with adjacent Henglan and Xiaolan towns, the cluster produces over 100 billion RMB annually. The Shenzhen–Zhongshan Link (opened June 2024) cut travel from Shenzhen from 2 hours to 30 minutes, making Guzhen a day trip from anywhere in the Pearl River Delta.

Nanhai district is China’s production base for commercial LED panels, downlights, and track lighting

Foshan’s Nanhai district, particularly Danzao town, is a major production centre for commercial and industrial LED lighting. Key manufacturers include Lianpu Lighting (30,000+ sqm factory), Mercanvee (established 1996, OEM for global brands), and Yingpeng Lighting (established 1997, 15,000 sqm). The cluster specialises in LED panels, recessed downlights, track lighting, and commercial fixtures with established export certifications.

Foshan’s advantage over Guzhen is in the mid-to-high-end commercial segment. Where Guzhen dominates decorative and residential lighting, Foshan factories tend to produce standardised commercial products with consistent quality for export. The city’s broader manufacturing ecosystem (aluminium, ceramics, building materials) means lighting manufacturers here have easy access to high-grade metal housings and heat sinks.

China’s smart lighting hub — WiFi, Bluetooth, Zigbee controllers and IoT-integrated systems

Shenzhen hosts over 70% of China’s LED manufacturers, driven by the city’s mature electronics supply chain and component infrastructure. Key districts include Guangming (high-efficiency LED factories), Bao’an (smart lighting systems and IoT integration), and Nanshan (R&D centres). The Huaqiangbei electronics market provides unparalleled access to LED components, controllers, and drivers — reducing lead times for prototyping and small-batch production.

Shenzhen’s competitive advantage is in connected and intelligent lighting: WiFi, Bluetooth, and Zigbee controllers, app-controlled systems, Tuya-compatible smart bulbs, LED strip controllers, and cloud-based lighting management. If you need lighting that connects to a phone app or integrates with Alexa and Google Home, Shenzhen is where the R&D happens. For decorative fixtures or bulk commodity LED products, Guzhen and Foshan offer better value.

Industrial, outdoor, and automotive LED — complementing Shenzhen’s tech and Guzhen’s decorative clusters

Dongguan’s Songshan Lake High-tech Zone and Huangjiang town host LED manufacturers focused on industrial, outdoor, and automotive lighting. Notable companies include MasonLED (100,000+ sqm, 800+ employees), Leysun Light (landscape and architectural fixtures since 2007), and GS LIGHT (31,000+ sqm, partnerships with Meanwell, Philips, Osram, Cree). TCL Lighting also operates multiple production bases in Dongguan.

Dongguan’s role in lighting is complementary rather than standalone. It is not a destination for decorative lighting or smart home products, but fills the gap for heavy-duty LED fixtures: high bay lights, flood lights, street lights, tri-proof lights, and automotive LED. Its proximity to Shenzhen (30 minutes) and Guzhen (1 hour via the new bridge) means many buyers combine visits.

Home to GILE (3,100+ exhibitors) and Canton Fair Phase 1 — the trade show hub, not the factory floor

Guangzhou’s role in lighting is primarily as a trade show and distribution hub. The Guangzhou International Lighting Exhibition (GILE), held at the Canton Fair Complex each June, is Asia’s largest lighting trade fair with 3,100+ exhibitors from 20+ countries across 250,000 sqm. Canton Fair Phase 1 (April) covers lighting in Area C. Some wholesale markets in Guangzhou carry lighting products, but for factory-direct pricing, Guzhen is 30–50% cheaper.

For buyers, Guangzhou’s value is access and comparison shopping rather than manufacturing. GILE and Canton Fair let you see thousands of suppliers in a few days, collect samples, and negotiate. But the factories behind those booths are in Guzhen (decorative), Foshan (commercial), and Shenzhen (smart). Think of Guangzhou as the showroom and the PRD cities as the workshop.

Export-grade standardised LED fixtures with direct port access — YUSING, KLITE, and Liaoyuan

Ningbo focuses on standardised LED products for export markets. Major manufacturers include YUSING Lighting (100,000 sqm, established 2002), Liaoyuan Lighting (established 1984, street lights and outdoor), Sunshinelux (91% export rate, 40 km from Ningbo Port), and KLITE (4 factories, 292,000 sqm total). Yuyao City’s Liangnong Town holds the official ‘Hometown of Chinese Lamps’ designation.

Ningbo’s competitive advantage is the combination of manufacturing and logistics. The port handles massive container volumes, which means lower per-unit shipping costs on large orders. Products tend to be mid-range, certification-ready LED fixtures rather than decorative or designer lighting. If you are buying 10,000+ standardised LED panels, street lights, or industrial fixtures for export, Ningbo’s factories plus port proximity is hard to beat.

How to source lighting from China

Four ways to find lighting suppliers. Most successful importers use multiple approaches at different stages.

Search on 1688

1688 is Alibaba's domestic B2B marketplace — where Chinese factories trade with each other. For lighting, that means factory-gate pricing on LED fixtures, components, and smart lighting systems that Alibaba.com doesn't surface. Start at light.1688.com, the dedicated lighting channel.

How to find lighting suppliers on 1688

1
Search in Chinese

Use Chinese product terms like 古镇灯饰 (Guzhen decorative lighting), 中山LED灯具 (Zhongshan LED fixtures), 古镇吸顶灯 (Guzhen ceiling lights), or 智能灯 (smart lights).

2
Filter by lighting hubs

Set location to 中山 (Zhongshan), 佛山 (Foshan), or 深圳 (Shenzhen). Use light.1688.com as your entry point for the dedicated lighting channel.

3
Check factory badges

Look for 实力商家 (Power Merchant) and 源头工厂 (Source Factory). For lighting, verify they list production capabilities — not just trading. Guzhen and Henglan addresses indicate real factories.

4
Verify before paying

Cross-check the business registration at gsxt.gov.cn. Use WorldFirst to pay in RMB directly to the supplier’s 1688/Alipay account. Pay through 1688 World Pay for buyer protection.
