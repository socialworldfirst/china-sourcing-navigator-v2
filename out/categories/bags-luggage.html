Bags & luggage manufacturing in China

China manufactures over 70% of the world's bags and luggage. In 2024, export value for travel goods and handbags reached US$20.7 billion — more than the next five largest exporting countries combined. The industry spans four major production belts: Guangdong (leather goods, handbags, fashion bags), Zhejiang (hard-case luggage, backpacks), Hebei (budget trolley cases and school bags), and Fujian (sports bags, outdoor gear).

What separates China from competitors like India, Vietnam, or Turkey is not just scale — it is supply chain compression. In Guangzhou's Shiling town, over 8,800 leather goods enterprises and 17,000 supporting merchants cluster within a single district. Raw leather, PU, hardware, zippers, lining fabric, and finished goods all sit within a 10 km radius. Orders can go from specification to finished product in hours, not weeks. That density is why Shiling alone accounts for 40% of China's entire leather goods transaction volume.

China’s leather capital — 8,800 factories, 7 billion products a year, 40% of national transaction volume

Shiling town in Huadu district is the undisputed centre of China’s leather goods industry. Over 8,800 production enterprises and 17,000 supporting merchants produce approximately 7 billion bags, belts, wallets, and cases annually. Products reach 142 countries, capturing over 60% of the mainstream luggage market in Europe and North America. The Shiling International Leather City covers 800,000 square metres with 4,200+ shops. Every year, the China (Shiling) Leather and Leather Goods Festival draws 150,000+ industry professionals and generates over 1 billion yuan in transactions per session.

The trading side lives in Sanyuanli, Baiyun district — closer to the city centre, easier for buyers doing short trips. The Guihuagang wholesale area alone has 24 leather markets producing 1.5 million pieces daily across 5,000+ brands. Baiyun World Leather Trading Centre on Jiefang North Road houses 1,200+ leather goods companies and 31,800 product categories. Factory pricing in Shiling runs 30–40% below Sanyuanli trading hub prices, but Sanyuanli offers walkable variety, same-day pickup, and proximity to Guangzhou Railway Station.

Sports bags, backpacks, and outdoor gear — factory-direct OEM with 15+ years of export experience

Jinjiang and Licheng districts host a growing cluster of bag and luggage factories, most with 15+ years of export experience. The product range covers backpacks, travel bags, luggage, cooler bags, sports bags, and school bags. Typical factories run 200–400 workers with strong OEM/ODM capability. Key companies include Dankinbags (~320 workers, BSCI/SEDEX/ISO9000/SA8000 certified), Xinwang Bags (10+ years), Flywon (15+ years), and Quanzhou Worldwide Bags.

The proximity to raw material suppliers is what makes Quanzhou competitive. Textiles, hardware, zippers (SBS Zipper Science & Technology is based in Jinjiang), and webbing all sit within a 50 km radius — the same supply chain that feeds the footwear cluster. Xiamen airport is 45 minutes away, and Xiamen port handles direct container shipping to Europe and the Americas. For buyers who want functional bags — sports, outdoor, travel, school — at factory-gate prices with genuine audit credentials, Quanzhou is the strongest option outside Guangdong.

District 2, Floor 1 — the bag section of the world’s largest commodity market

Yiwu International Trade City’s bag section sits on the 1st and 4th floors of District 2, with 8,000+ booths open 365 days a year. About 75% of suppliers are factory-direct sales offices. The market covers everything from lady’s handbags to school bags, wallets, cosmetics cases, and trolley suitcases. MOQs can drop as low as 20–50 pieces per style for stock items. Most suppliers represent factories from Wenzhou, Guangzhou, Baigou (Hebei), and Shaodong (Hunan).

Yiwu’s strength is logistics, not manufacturing. The city’s freight network ships to 230+ countries, and consolidation services make it easy to combine bags with other small commodities in one container. For small-to-medium importers buying mixed containers of finished bags at the lowest possible price point, Yiwu offers the widest selection per square metre in China.

Mid-to-premium export bags and luggage — where international brands place contract orders

Dongguan’s bag and luggage factories grew from the same Taiwanese and Hong Kong investment that built its shoe industry in the 1990s. Companies like Sitoy Group (listed on HKEX, manufactures for Coach, Samsonite, Tumi) anchor the premium end. Multiple factories in Houjie, Zhongtang, and Humen produce EVA hard cases, laptop bags, travel bags, and fashion handbags for export. Quality control infrastructure, port access (Dongguan Port at Shatian), and proximity to Hong Kong make logistics predictable.

Dongguan suits buyers targeting the mid-to-high end. Factories here typically require MOQs of 300–1,000 pieces per style and specialise in complex construction: structured bags, multi-compartment laptop cases, spinner luggage with TSA locks. Many factories refuse orders below US$10 per unit as they move upmarket. For budget bags, Yiwu or Baigou are more appropriate.

Trade fairs, compliance labs, and the world’s busiest container port

Shanghai is not a bag manufacturing city, but it is where the industry’s trade infrastructure sits. The All China Leather Exhibition (ACLE) draws 800+ exhibitors from 36 countries and 23,000 buyers every September at SNIEC. The Shanghai International Bags Exhibition (June 2026) is another key B2B platform for the luggage and accessories sector. For importers focused on leather goods, materials, and machinery, Shanghai’s trade fairs are the most international in China.

Yangshan Deep Water Port handles more container volume than any port in the world. Bags manufactured in Guangzhou, Quanzhou, or Baigou frequently ship through Shanghai when heading to the US East Coast or Europe. Shanghai also has the deepest pool of third-party testing labs (SGS, Bureau Veritas, Intertek) for bag compliance testing — essential for US CPSIA, EU REACH, and Australian labelling requirements.

Also important: Baigou, Hebei

Baigou town (Baoding, Hebei) is China's largest luggage production base by volume — 800+ million bags sold annually, 35% domestic market share, 350 large-scale enterprises, and exports to 195 countries. It specialises in budget-to-mid-range trolley cases, school bags, and travel bags. Baigou is not currently in the Navigator's 20-city list, but it is a major sourcing destination for volume luggage buyers.

How to source bags & luggage from China

Four ways to find bag and luggage suppliers. Most successful importers use multiple approaches at different stages.

Search on 1688

1688 is Alibaba's domestic B2B marketplace — where Chinese factories trade with each other. For bags and luggage, that means factory-gate pricing on handbags, backpacks, suitcases, and leather goods that Alibaba.com doesn't surface. Filter by 狮岭 (Shiling) for leather bags or 白沟 (Baigou) for trolley luggage.

How to find bag suppliers on 1688

1
Search in Chinese

Use Chinese product terms like 筥包批发 (bags wholesale), 手袋厂家 (handbag factory), 行李箱厂家 (luggage factory), or 皮具厂家 (leather goods factory).

2
Filter by bag production hubs

Set location to 狮岭 (Shiling) for leather bags, 白沟 (Baigou) for trolley luggage, 晋江 (Jinjiang) for sports bags, or 平湖 (Pinghu) for hard-case suitcases.

3
Check factory badges

Look for 超级工厂 (Super Factory) and 实力商家 (Power Merchant). Check repeat buyer rate (回头率) — 20%+ signals consistent quality.

4
Verify before paying

Cross-check the business registration on Tianyancha. Use WorldFirst to pay in RMB directly to the supplier’s 1688/Alipay account.
