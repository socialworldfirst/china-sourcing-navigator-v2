Sports equipment manufacturing in China

China produces 76% of the world's gym and fitness equipment by volume — more than ten times the output of the United States. In 2024, China exported US$28.4 billion in sporting goods, with fitness equipment alone reaching US$5.05 billion (+22% year on year). Over 63,000 sports equipment enterprises generate a combined industry output exceeding 1.5 trillion yuan.

What makes China's sports supply chain different from competitors is geographic specialisation. Jinjiang (Quanzhou) is the global sports brands capital — home to Anta, Xtep, and 361 Degrees, producing 10 billion pairs of sneakers a year. Xiamen is Asia's largest export base for fitness and massage equipment — six out of ten home treadmills on earth start there. Ningjin County in Shandong commands 70% of China's commercial gym equipment market. Qingdao dominates rubber fitness products. Each city owns a different part of the sports supply chain.

quanzhou

Sports brands capital of China — Anta, Xtep, 361 Degrees HQ, 5,000+ shoe producers, 10 billion sneakers a year

Jinjiang, a county-level city under Quanzhou in Fujian, is the undisputed sports brands capital of China. Three of China’s four largest sportswear companies are headquartered here: Anta Sports (62.36 billion yuan revenue in 2023, surpassing both Nike China and Adidas China), Xtep International, and 361 Degrees International. The city has over 5,000 shoe producers turning out roughly 10 billion pairs of sneakers annually.

Jinjiang’s footwear and clothing industry output exceeded 300 billion yuan (US$41.4 billion) in 2022, and the city’s GDP hit 336.35 billion yuan in 2023 — Fujian’s highest-GDP county-level entity for over 15 consecutive years. The supply chain is vertically integrated: factories, EVA and rubber component suppliers, mould makers, and design centres sit within kilometres of each other. Many factories also serve as OEM producers for international brands including Nike, Adidas, and Puma.

xiamen

Asia’s largest export base for fitness and massage equipment — six out of ten home treadmills globally come from Xiamen

Xiamen is Asia’s largest production and export base for sports, fitness, and massage therapy equipment. Six out of ten home treadmills sold worldwide are manufactured in Xiamen. K-Power Sports produces over 600 varieties of fitness equipment with 70% destined for export. Everesports (established 1998) exports treadmills, bikes, rowing machines, and strength equipment to 80+ countries. Tongda Smart Tech covers 40+ categories including jump ropes, silicone water bottles, ice skates, and archery gear.

Fujian province exported 7 billion yuan in sports goods in the first seven months of 2024 alone (+15.41% year on year), with exports to RCEP member countries surging 31%. Xiamen also manufactures carbon fibre tennis rackets and composite sports products. The port of Xiamen provides direct export access, and the city hosts the annual China Sport Show — Asia’s largest sporting goods trade event (1,600+ exhibitors, 120,000+ visitors).

ningbo

Dense fitness equipment cluster with world’s busiest port — treadmills, gym accessories, and sports hardware

Ningbo hosts a dense cluster of fitness equipment manufacturers specialising in treadmills, exercise bikes, and gym accessories. Ningbo New Noble Sport Equipment has 30 years of manufacturing experience in commercial and home treadmills. Ningbo Qisheng has 18 years as a treadmill specialist with ISO 9001 certification. Tianjian Fitness and Jada Health Tech round out the cluster with multi-gym and cardio equipment production.

Ningbo-Zhoushan Port — the world’s busiest by cargo tonnage — sits minutes from most factories, making it ideal for shipping heavy gym equipment. The broader Zhejiang region excels in compact fitness equipment production with a flexible supply chain. Ningbo also produces sports accessories, outdoor products, and small fitness gear, making it a strong one-stop option for buyers combining equipment with accessories.

dongguan

OEM/ODM contract manufacturing — roller skates, helmets, neoprene supports, sports garment accessories

Dongguan’s sports equipment cluster grew from the same Hong Kong and Taiwanese investment that built its toy and electronics industries. Chen Tai Yu Sports Equipment produces ice skates, racing shoes, roller skates, helmets, and ski gear. Yonghui Sporting Goods (established 1996) manufactures the DunRun brand product line. JiaNan (established 2000) is one of the largest garment and sport equipment accessory factories in China.

Hui Xin Sports Products in Liao Bu specialises in neoprene products for sports and medical protection. SGL Sports produces high-end sportswear from a 3,500-square-metre factory with 200+ skilled workers. Dongguan’s strength is contract manufacturing with strong QC infrastructure — ideal for international brands placing OEM/ODM orders with strict specifications. The city sits 30 minutes from Shenzhen’s Yantian port and an hour from Hong Kong.

qingdao

Rubber fitness equipment hub — 3,000 tonnes of bumper plates a year, plus martial arts gear and artificial turf

Qingdao is China’s rubber fitness equipment capital. DH Sport Equipment produces 3,000 tonnes of rubber bumper plates, rubber-coated dumbbells, and related products annually, with over US$8 million in sales all destined for Europe and America. Modun Industry specialises in rubber bumper plates, cast iron plates, hex dumbbells, kettlebells, barbells, benches, and power racks from a facility with 60 high-temperature vulcanising machines.

CSP Industry (established 2010) covers artificial turf, rubber mats, and fitness equipment across multiple production bases. Qingdao is also a core zone for martial arts equipment — Tide Sports manufactures taekwondo, karate, boxing, and BJJ equipment alongside EVA/XPE foam floor mats. The Qingdao port provides direct access to North American and European markets, and the city’s rubber processing heritage from tyre manufacturing feeds directly into fitness product quality.

How to source sports equipment from China

Four ways to find sports equipment suppliers. Most successful importers use multiple approaches at different stages.

Search on 1688

1688 is Alibaba's domestic B2B marketplace — where Chinese factories trade with each other. For sports equipment, that means factory-gate pricing on treadmills, dumbbells, yoga mats, and gym machines that Alibaba.com doesn't surface. Filter by 厦门 (Xiamen) for fitness machines or 青岛 (Qingdao) for rubber weights.

How to find sports equipment suppliers on 1688

1
Search in Chinese

Use Chinese product terms like 运动器材 (sports equipment), 健身器材工厂 (fitness equipment factory), 哑铃厂家 (dumbbell factory), or 瑜伽垫批发 (yoga mat wholesale).

2
Filter by sports equipment hubs

Set location to 厦门 (Xiamen) for treadmills and cardio, 宁波 (Ningbo) for gym accessories, 青岛 (Qingdao) for rubber weights, or 晋江 (Jinjiang) for sports footwear and apparel.

3
Check factory badges

Look for 超级工厂 (Super Factory) and 实力商家 (Power Merchant). For fitness equipment, ISO 9001 and CE certification mentions in the shop description indicate export-ready factories.

4
Verify before paying

Cross-check the business registration on Tianyancha. Heavy equipment carries high shipping risk — request video of machines running before ordering. Use WorldFirst to pay in RMB directly to the supplier’s 1688/Alipay account.
