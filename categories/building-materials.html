Building materials manufacturing in China

China produces roughly 44% of the world's ceramic tiles — 5.9 billion square metres in 2024 alone. Foshan, in Guangdong province, runs over 1,000 production lines and accounts for more than half of that national output. The country has held the number-one position in ceramic tile exports for 32 consecutive years, shipping 599 million square metres worth US$3.2 billion in 2024.

Beyond ceramics, China dominates stone processing (Nan'an handles 60% of national exports), sanitary ware (US$15.6 billion in exports, 52.5% of world volume), and plumbing components. The supply chain is concentrated in a handful of specialist cities — Foshan for tiles and aluminium, Quanzhou for stone and plumbing, and Guangzhou as the trading and logistics gateway.

Detailed breakdowns for the top building materials sourcing cities

World ceramics capital — 54% of China’s tile output, 1,000+ production lines, 5,000 years of ceramic heritage

Foshan has been making ceramics for five millennia. The modern industry took off in the 1980s when Italian production line technology was imported, and today the city runs over 1,000 lines producing 1.2 billion square metres of tiles annually. That is roughly 54% of China’s total and about a quarter of global output. The trading hub is Nanzhuang in Chancheng district, designated “China Building Ceramic First Town.”

Beyond tiles, Foshan’s building materials ecosystem includes Huayi International Decoration Expo City (330,000 sqm, 2,000 shops covering tiles, flooring, doors, windows, sanitaryware, hardware, paint), Casa Ceramics & Sanitaryware Mall (650+ suppliers, est. 1994), and Meiju International Building Materials Expo (280,000 sqm). Dali town adds China’s largest aluminium profile cluster for window frames and facades.

60% of China’s stone exports, 70% of plumbing valve market share, 2,000+ stone enterprises in Shuitou alone

Nan’an’s Shuitou town is the world’s largest stone processing and trading hub. Over 2,000 stone enterprises, 100,000+ workers, and 60% of China’s stone exports pass through here. The Shuitou International Stone Fair is the world’s third-largest stone exhibition. Raw materials arrive from Italy, Turkey, India, and dozens of other countries for processing and re-export to 140+ markets.

On the plumbing side, Luncang town (“China’s Plumbing Township”) and Yingdu town (“China’s Plumbing Valve Base Town”) host 700+ sanitary ware enterprises with 100,000+ workers and 70%+ national market share for plumbing valves. One in three taps sold in China is Nan’an-made. Brands include HHSN/Huihuang (est. 1988) and JOYOU (est. 1979).

Trade gateway — wholesale markets, Canton Fair Phase 2, and the logistics hub connecting Foshan factories to the world

Guangzhou functions as the commercial front door for building materials sourced from Foshan and the wider Pearl River Delta. Anhua Decoration Material Centre (est. 1993, 60,000+ sqm, 1,000+ booths) covers lighting, flooring, wooden doors, aluminium windows, kitchen fittings, and sanitary products. The Guangdong Decoration Material Market adds another million-plus square metres.

Canton Fair Phase 2 (23–27 April) dedicates entire halls to tiles, flooring, doors, windows, bathroom fixtures, sanitary ware, and decorative building materials. For buyers already visiting Foshan factories, Guangzhou is 30 minutes by high-speed rail and serves as the logistics coordination point for container consolidation and freight forwarding.

Fujian’s port gateway for stone exports — proximity to Nan’an’s processing clusters

Xiamen is the primary export port for Nan’an’s stone products and hosts the annual Xiamen International Stone Fair, one of the world’s four largest stone exhibitions. The city’s logistics infrastructure connects directly to the Quanzhou-Shuitou processing zone, making it the departure point for most of China’s processed stone exports.

Design-led market — premium imported stone, high-end bathroom fittings, international brand showrooms

Shanghai is less a production hub and more a design and distribution centre for premium building materials. International brand showrooms, architect-specified imported stone, and high-end bathroom fittings dominate. Shanghai Kitchen & Bath Show (KBC) and Design Shanghai attract global brands and specification buyers.

Four ways to find building materials suppliers. Most successful importers combine online discovery with in-person market visits and factory tours.

1688 is Alibaba's domestic B2B marketplace — where Chinese factories trade with each other. For building materials, that means factory-direct pricing on tiles, sanitaryware, stone, plumbing fittings, and construction hardware that Alibaba.com doesn't show you.

How to find building materials suppliers on 1688

1
Search in Chinese

Use product names in Chinese: 瓷砖 (ceramic tiles), 卫浴 (sanitary ware), 石材 (stone), 建材 (building materials), 水暖 (plumbing fittings). WorldFirst’s AI Sourcing Agent translates for you.

2
Filter by production hubs

Set the supplier location filter to Foshan (佛山) for tiles and ceramics, Nan’an (南安) for stone and plumbing, or Guangdong (广东) province for broad coverage.

3
Check factory badges

Look for 实力商家 (verified merchant) and 源头工厂 (source factory) tags. For tiles, check whether they list their own kiln lines. For stone, check if they have their own processing plant.

4
Verify before paying

Cross-check the business registration. Pay through 1688 World Pay for CNY payment protection. Building materials involve heavy, fragile goods — confirm packaging and logistics capability before committing.
