Appliance manufacturing in China

China exported 4.48 billion household appliances in 2024 — a 20.8% increase over the previous year — and produced 5.3 billion units worth US$143.4 billion. Guangdong province alone accounts for over 40% of national output, with Foshan's Shunde district recognised as the “hub of Chinese home appliances” and home to Midea, Galanz, and thousands of OEM/ODM factories.

In Zhejiang, the county-level city of Cixi near Ningbo produces roughly 60% of the world's small home appliances — electric irons, heaters, hair dryers, air fryers, and sandwich makers — backed by 2,000+ manufacturers and nearly 10,000 component suppliers. Qingdao in Shandong anchors the major white goods segment, with Haier and Hisense headquartered there alongside 2,200 cluster enterprises. Whether you need a single SKU of air fryers or a full private-label kitchen range, China has a production belt purpose-built for your order.

Cixi county — the world’s largest small appliance production base, supplying 60% of global output

Ningbo’s Cixi county is the single most important city in the world for small home appliances. The cluster houses over 2,000 appliance manufacturers and nearly 10,000 supporting enterprises, covering more than 20 product series and thousands of SKUs. If you want to manufacture a small appliance in Cixi, you can find at least 99% of the components within a 30-minute drive — that kind of supply chain density does not exist anywhere else for this category.

Cixi dominates specific product lines at a global level. Annual output of electric irons exceeds 60 million units, accounting for over 60% of national export volume. Heaters exceed 25 million units, representing over 30% of national production. Hair dryers are a national “single champion” product. The cluster also produces air fryers, sandwich makers, bread makers, hanging steamers, and garment care devices. Notable brands from Cixi include Bull (公牛) for electrical accessories and Fotile (方太) for kitchen hoods, while the bulk of factories serve international brands like Philips, Panasonic, and Siemens as OEM partners.

For importers, Ningbo is the natural starting point for small kitchen appliances, personal care devices, and heating/cooling products. The port of Ningbo-Zhoushan — the world’s busiest by cargo tonnage — sits right there, meaning your goods go from factory floor to container ship with minimal inland logistics cost.

Shunde — the “hub of Chinese home appliances” and headquarters of Midea, Galanz, and 3,000+ appliance firms

Foshan’s Shunde district has been called the “hub of Chinese home appliances” since the 1990s, and the numbers back it up. Over 3,000 appliance enterprises cluster here, and the district’s output captures 25% of the global rice cooker market, 33% of range hoods, 43% of water heaters, and 48% of microwave ovens. Midea Group, the world’s largest appliance company by revenue, is headquartered in Beijiao town, Shunde, with multiple manufacturing campuses within the district.

Galanz, which makes close to half the world’s microwave ovens, operates production bases in both Zhongshan and Shunde. Bear Electric (小熊电器), the fast-growing small appliance brand popular with younger Chinese consumers, is also Shunde-born. The district covers the full value chain — from motor winding and sheet metal stamping to injection moulding, PCB assembly, and final product testing — all within a compact area.

For importers sourcing kitchen appliances (rice cookers, air fryers, induction hobs, range hoods, microwave ovens), water heaters, or major white goods, Shunde is the primary destination. Factories here have deep experience with international certifications (UL, CE, CB) and can handle OEM runs from 500 units up to container-load volumes.

Home to Haier and Hisense — China’s only national innovation centre for home appliance manufacturing

Qingdao is the white goods capital of northern China. Haier and Hisense are both headquartered here, and the city has been approved as China’s only national innovation centre for home appliance manufacturing. The cluster includes over 2,200 enterprises, 17 national manufacturing champion companies, and 1,297 high-tech enterprises. From January to November 2023, the cluster produced nearly 60 million units of refrigerators, freezers, air conditioners, washing machines, and televisions — over 9% of China’s total output.

Qingdao’s appliance exports reached RMB 31.05 billion in the first 11 months of 2023, up 22.2% year-on-year. The city has a particular strength in “smart” appliances — Haier operates multiple “lighthouse factories” here, recognised by the World Economic Forum for their advanced manufacturing systems. For importers interested in large appliances (refrigerators, washing machines, freezers) or smart home integration, Qingdao offers the most advanced production capabilities in China.

Canton Fair host city and southern China’s largest appliance wholesale distribution hub

Guangzhou is not where most appliances are manufactured — that happens in Foshan (30 minutes west) and the broader PRD. But Guangzhou is where they are traded. The biannual Canton Fair, held in Pazhou, is the single largest trade event for home appliances globally, with Phase 1 covering household electrical appliances alongside electronics and hardware. Thousands of appliance exhibitors from Shunde, Zhongshan, and across Guangdong show here.

Beyond the Canton Fair, Guangzhou has wholesale markets and showrooms for kitchen and home appliances scattered across Tianhe and Baiyun districts. The city’s role is as a trade orchestration point — you meet suppliers here, negotiate, then visit factories in Foshan, Zhongshan, or Dongguan. For a first sourcing trip, Guangzhou gives you the widest exposure to the appliance supply chain in the shortest time.

3,500+ appliance enterprises across Nantou, Dongfeng, and Huangpu — China’s first town-level home appliance base

Zhongshan operates on the Pearl River Delta’s unique “one industry, one town” model. Nantou Town was recognised in 2004 as China’s first town-level “Chinese home appliance industry base,” and the broader city now has over 3,500 home appliance enterprises and supporting companies. Dongfeng Town specialises in electric household appliances, while Xiaolan handles locks, hardware, and electronic acoustics.

Zhongshan is particularly strong in water heaters, small cooking appliances, and ventilation equipment. Galanz has production facilities here. For importers, Zhongshan is worth visiting alongside Foshan — the cities are adjacent, and Zhongshan factories often quote slightly below Shunde equivalents due to lower land and labour costs.

How to source appliances from China

Four ways to find appliance suppliers. Most successful importers use multiple approaches at different stages.

Search on 1688

1688 is Alibaba's domestic B2B marketplace — where Chinese factories trade with each other. For appliances, that means factory-direct pricing, real MOQs, and access to Cixi and Shunde suppliers that don't list on Alibaba.com.

How to find appliance suppliers on 1688

1
Search in Chinese

Use product names in Chinese: 家电 (home appliances), 小家电 (small appliances), 电饭煲 (rice cooker), 空气炸锞 (air fryer), 吹风机 (hair dryer), 微波炉 (microwave oven).

2
Filter by appliance hubs

Set the supplier location to Cixi/Ningbo (慈溪/宁波) for small appliances, Foshan/Shunde (佛山/顺德) for kitchen appliances, or Qingdao (青岛) for major white goods.

3
Check factory badges

Look for 实力商家 (verified merchant) and 源头工厂 (source factory). For appliances, also check if they list ISO 9001 and relevant export certifications (UL, CE, CB).

4
Verify before paying

Cross-check the business registration and any certification claims. Pay through 1688 World Pay for CNY payment protection.

The biannual Canton Fair, held in Pazhou, is the single largest trade event for home appliances globally, with Phase 1 covering household electrical appliances alongside electronics and hardware. Thousands of appliance exhibitors from Shunde, Zhongshan, and across Guangdong show here.

Beyond the Canton Fair, Guangzhou has wholesale markets and showrooms for kitchen and home appliances scattered across Tianhe and Baiyun districts. The city’s role is as a trade orchestration point — you meet suppliers here, negotiate, then visit factories in Foshan, Zhongshan, or Dongguan. For a first sourcing trip, Guangzhou gives you the widest exposure to the appliance supply chain in the shortest time.

3,500+ appliance enterprises across Nantou, Dongfeng, and Huangpu — China’s first town-level home appliance base

Zhongshan operates on the Pearl River Delta’s unique “one industry, one town” model. Nantou Town was recognised in 2004 as China’s first town-level “Chinese home appliance industry base,” and the broader city now has over 3,500 home appliance enterprises and supporting companies. Dongfeng Town specialises in electric household appliances, while Xiaolan handles locks, hardware, and electronic acoustics.

Zhongshan is particularly strong in water heaters, small cooking appliances, and ventilation equipment. Galanz has production facilities here. For importers, Zhongshan is worth visiting alongside Foshan — the cities are adjacent, and Zhongshan factories often quote slightly below Shunde equivalents due to lower land and labour costs.

1688 is Alibaba's domestic B2B marketplace — where Chinese factories trade with each other. For appliances, that means factory-direct pricing, real MOQs, and access to Cixi and Shunde suppliers that don't list on Alibaba.com.

How to find appliance suppliers on 1688

1
Search in Chinese

Use product names in Chinese: 家电 (home appliances), 小家电 (small appliances), 电饭煲 (rice cooker), 空气炸锞 (air fryer), 吹风机 (hair dryer), 微波炉 (microwave oven).

2
Filter by appliance hubs

Set the supplier location to Cixi/Ningbo (慈溪/宁波) for small appliances, Foshan/Shunde (佛山/顺德) for kitchen appliances, or Qingdao (青岛) for major white goods.

3
Check factory badges

Look for 实力商家 (verified merchant) and 源头工厂 (source factory). For appliances, also check if they list ISO 9001 and relevant export certifications (UL, CE, CB).

4
Verify before paying

Cross-check the business registration and any certification claims. Pay through 1688 World Pay for CNY payment protection.
