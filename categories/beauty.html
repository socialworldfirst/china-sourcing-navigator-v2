Beauty & Cosmetics Manufacturing in China

China produces over 30% of the world's cosmetics. Guangdong province alone accounts for nearly two-thirds of national production capacity, with Baiyun district in Guangzhou hosting one quarter of all certified factories. Exports reached $6.53 billion in 2024 and are growing at double digits for the third year running.

The supply chain runs through distinct stages: raw ingredients from Shandong and Yunnan, R&D and premium formulation in Shanghai's Oriental Beauty Valley, mass production and filling in Guangzhou's Baiyun district, packaging from Yuyao and Shenzhen, and export through Guangzhou customs. For private-label brands, concept to container ship takes as little as 45 days.

1,300 licensed factories in Baiyun district — one quarter of China’s entire cosmetics output

Guangzhou is the undisputed cosmetics capital of China. Guangdong province accounts for over 63% of national production capacity, and Baiyun District sits at the heart of it all — approximately 1,300 certified cosmetics production enterprises, 25% of the national total, and one third of all general cosmetics product filings (450,000). The district operates on a “北产南贸” (north production, south trade) model. Northern towns — Junhe (156 enterprises), Jianggao (~140), and Longgui (264 manufacturers plus ~1,000 upstream suppliers) — hold 90%+ of the factories. Concept to container ship takes as few as 15 days, versus a 45-day industry average, because R&D labs, ingredient suppliers, filling lines, packaging factories, and testing centres are all within a 30-minute drive.

Guangzhou’s wholesale markets anchor the buying side. Meibo City (广州美博城) is Asia’s largest beauty wholesale centre — 110,000 sqm, 1,500+ stores, ~RMB 10 billion annual turnover. Xingfa Plaza (1,427 shops) is strictly wholesale, factory-direct, and will not sell single items. EVA International is one of China’s top three press-on nail wholesale hubs. All three sit near Sanyuanli metro station. You can visit factories in the morning and wholesale markets in the afternoon without leaving the district.

Oriental Beauty Valley — where China’s premium cosmetics are formulated

While Guangzhou dominates on volume, Shanghai leads on premium formulation, multinational R&D, and regulatory compliance. Fengxian District’s Oriental Beauty Valley houses 84 of Shanghai’s 226 licensed cosmetics manufacturers in a dedicated 1.5 sq km zone, accounting for 40%+ of the city’s total cosmetics sales revenue. Global contract manufacturers operate here: Cosmax (3 factories, 200M products/year capacity), Intercos (supplies 40% of the world’s high-end brands including Dior, Lancome, Chanel, and Armani), alongside Estee Lauder, L’Oreal, and Pechoin operations.

Shanghai factories charge 15–25% more than Guangzhou, but compliance documentation is notably better — ideal for brands targeting EU or US markets where regulatory rigour matters. The China Beauty Expo (CBE) runs annually in May with 3,200+ exhibitors including 1,500+ OEM/ODM, ingredients, packaging, and equipment companies. For premium positioning, anti-aging peptides, sensitive-skin formulations, or clean beauty, Shanghai is the starting point.

70% of the world’s false eyelashes are made in Pingdu

Qingdao’s Pingdu district is a world-class beauty niche that most sourcing guides miss entirely. This is not a general cosmetics hub — it is the undisputed global capital of false eyelashes. The numbers: 5,000+ registered eyelash companies, 20,000+ processing units, ~60,000 employees, US$1.4 billion annual output, and 3,000+ styles covering full strip lashes, eyelash extensions, fan eyelashes, magnetic, and cluster types. The heritage runs 40+ years, with the first factory opening in 1976 in Dazeshan township. Key production townships are Dazeshan (大泽山), Jiudian (旧店), and Xinhe (新河) in Pingdu district.

MOQs start at 50–500 pairs for samples and 1,000+ for production runs. Pingdu is 1.5–2 hours from downtown Qingdao — rent a car or hire a driver. Larger factories (1,000+ sqm) welcome visitors and allow inspection of automated bonding lines, sterilisation chambers, and QC labs. Products reach 120+ countries, with the US taking 25%, Europe 15%, and Japan/South Korea 10%.

1,100+ beauty booths in the world’s largest small commodities market

Yiwu is not where cosmetics are made — it is where they are traded. The Yiwu International Trade City is the world’s largest small commodities market, and its beauty section is the go-to for buyers seeking low MOQs, mixed containers, and budget-friendly product. District 3, Floor 3 covers cosmetics, skincare, nail art, haircare, perfumes, cosmetic packaging, beauty tools, brushes, sponges, mirrors, and manicure sets. The district has 1,200 business entities with MOQs as low as 50–100 pieces per product. Best visit windows are March to May and September to November for new product launches and freshest inventory.

Yiwu is best for budget beauty, accessories, tools, and mixed containers. It is not the place for high-end formulation or custom OEM/ODM — that is Guangzhou. Yiwu is where you browse, compare, and buy ready-made at volume. Most vendors are experienced in export and shipping logistics. Hours run 08:30–17:30 in summer and 08:30–17:00 in winter.
