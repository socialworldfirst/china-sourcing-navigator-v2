Hardware & tools manufacturing in China

China accounts for roughly 80% of the world's power tool production by volume and over 37% of the global hand tools market. In 2024, Chinese factories produced 543 million power tools — up 19% year on year — and exported 387 million units worth US$10.9 billion. On the fasteners side, China shipped 4.98 million tonnes of bolts, screws, and nuts valued at US$11.1 billion, making it the world's largest fastener exporter by a wide margin.

What makes China's hardware supply chain unique is geographic clustering. Yongkang (a county-level city under Jinhua, Zhejiang) is universally known as the hardware capital of China — home to 30,000+ enterprises and 1,700 power tool manufacturers alone. Ningbo, just two hours north-east, dominates fastener production with Haiyan County accounting for 9% of national fastener exports. Yiwu's District 2 hosts 3,000 hardware booths open 365 days a year. Each city owns a different slice of the hardware value chain.

ningbo

China’s fastener export powerhouse and gateway to Yongkang — the hardware capital with 30,000+ enterprises producing 100,000+ product types

Ningbo anchors the eastern end of China’s hardware belt. The city itself is a major hand tool and fastener manufacturing centre, with companies like Ningbo Fastenwell, Yonggang Fasteners (a Sino-US joint venture producing 20,000+ tonnes annually, 90% exported), and dozens of specialist wrench, plier, and socket set factories across Yinzhou and Cixi districts. Ningbo-Zhoushan Port — the world’s busiest by cargo tonnage — sits minutes from most factories, giving hardware exporters a direct logistics advantage.

Ningbo’s real superpower is proximity to two satellite clusters. Yongkang, a county-level city 2.5 hours south-west under Jinhua, is universally called the hardware capital of China. It hosts 30,000+ enterprises producing over 100,000 product types, including 1,700 power tool manufacturers, 650+ product categories, and 150,000 workers. The city’s industrial output reached 80 billion yuan. East of Ningbo, Haiyan County (under Jiaxing) is one of China’s top three fastener production bases — 400+ enterprises achieved output exceeding 12 billion yuan, with self-operated exports accounting for 9% of the national total. Buyers visiting Ningbo can cover all three clusters within a day’s drive.

yiwu

World’s largest small commodities market — 3,000 hardware booths in District 2, open 365 days, MOQs from 10 pieces

Yiwu International Trade City District 2 is the single largest walk-in hardware marketplace on earth. The second floor alone houses approximately 3,000 booths across sections G2 and F2, covering hand tools, power tools, cutting tools, locks, fittings, plumbing hardware, and electrical accessories. The ground floor adds more hardware, locks, and fastener stalls. The market operates 09:00–17:00, seven days a week, 365 days a year (except a 15-day Spring Festival closure).

Yiwu’s advantage for hardware buyers is flexibility. Stock items start from 10–100 pieces per SKU — a fraction of factory MOQs. Buyers can walk the market, collect samples from dozens of suppliers in a single day, and negotiate mixed-container orders combining hand tools, fasteners, and fittings. Many Yiwu booth holders are agents for Yongkang factories, so pricing is close to factory gate. For buyers not ready to commit to factory MOQs of 500–1,000 units, Yiwu is the natural starting point.

qingdao

Shandong’s fastener and industrial hardware hub — 3,000+ tonnes annual output, strong export infrastructure to Europe and North America

Qingdao is Shandong’s primary hardware export hub, with particular strength in fasteners and industrial-grade tools. Qingdao Xinhua Hardware (established 1987) produces 3,000+ tonnes of standard and non-standard fasteners annually using 100+ production machines. Qingdao Jinrui Fastener specialises in washers, nuts, and bolts exported to Germany, Greece, and across the Middle East. Qingdao Join Rising brings 20 years of experience in railway fasteners, hex bolts, and structural bolts.

Qingdao’s geographic position gives it a shipping advantage to European and North American markets — transit times from Qingdao port are typically 2–3 days shorter than from southern Chinese ports when heading to Northern Europe or the US East Coast. The city’s heavy industrial heritage (Haier, Hisense) means deep expertise in metal processing, heat treatment, and precision manufacturing. For buyers sourcing industrial fasteners, structural hardware, or marine-grade fittings, Qingdao offers a compelling combination of quality and logistics efficiency.

dongguan

Precision hardware and metal parts — CNC machining, die-casting, and OEM tool accessories for global brands

Dongguan’s role in hardware is less about finished tools and more about precision components that go into them. The city’s CNC machining, die-casting, and metal stamping infrastructure — built to serve electronics and automotive industries — translates directly to hardware fittings, tool components, and specialty fasteners. Qixin Hardware Tools Factory in Zhongtang specialises in allen keys, hex wrenches, and hardware hand tools.

Dongguan excels at OEM/ODM hardware work: custom brackets, hinges, handles, drawer slides, and precision metal parts produced to international specifications with tight tolerances. The city sits 30 minutes from Shenzhen’s Yantian port and an hour from Hong Kong, making it ideal for buyers who need precision hardware components with fast export access. Many global tool brands source metal components from Dongguan factories even when final assembly happens elsewhere.

guangzhou

Canton Fair host city and southern China’s largest hardware wholesale district — Tianping Architecture Hardware City

Guangzhou’s hardware strength centres on its role as Canton Fair host (Phase 1 covers hardware and tools every April and October) and several dedicated wholesale markets. Tianping Architecture Hardware City in Haizhu district is the largest hardware wholesale centre in southern China, covering architectural hardware, locks, hinges, handles, and building fittings. Xiran Hardware Co. specialises in high-end furniture and decorative hardware fittings.

For international buyers, Guangzhou functions as the trade show gateway. Canton Fair Phase 1 brings together thousands of hardware and tools exhibitors across categories including manual tools, electric tools, pneumatic tools, fasteners, locks, wire mesh, welding materials, and sundry hardware. Buyers who attend Canton Fair in Guangzhou can then extend their trip to Yongkang (2 hours by high-speed rail to Jinhua) or Yiwu (1.5 hours) to visit factories and markets directly.

How to source hardware & tools from China

Four ways to find hardware suppliers. Most successful importers use multiple approaches at different stages.

Search on 1688

1688 is Alibaba's domestic B2B marketplace — where Chinese factories trade with each other. For hardware, that means factory-gate pricing on wrenches, drills, fasteners, and fittings that Alibaba.com doesn't surface. Filter by 永康 (Yongkang) for power tools or 宁波 (Ningbo) for fasteners and hand tools.

How to find hardware & tools suppliers on 1688

1
Search in Chinese

Use Chinese product terms like 五金工具 (hardware tools), 电动工具 (power tools), 螺丝 (screws), 扳手 (wrenches), or 钳子厂家 (plier factory).

2
Filter by hardware hubs

Set location to 永康 (Yongkang) for power tools and hand tools, 宁波 (Ningbo) for fasteners and hand tools, or 义乌 (Yiwu) for mixed hardware at low MOQs.

3
Check factory badges

Look for 超级工厂 (Super Factory) and 实力商家 (Power Merchant). For tools, also check whether the supplier lists CrV steel grades and holds GS or CE certifications.

4
Verify before paying

Cross-check the business registration on Tianyancha. Request sample tools and test hardness, finish quality, and dimensional accuracy before committing to bulk. Use WorldFirst to pay in RMB directly.
