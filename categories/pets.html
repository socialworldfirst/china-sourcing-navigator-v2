Pet product manufacturing in China

China exported over US$4 billion in pet products in 2023, making it one of the world's largest suppliers of pet food, accessories, and toys. The industry spans two distinct belts: Zhejiang province dominates accessories and chews — Pingyang county alone accounts for more than half of China's pet chew exports and over 60% of global pet chew toy production — while Hebei's Nanhe district produces 60% of China's dry pet food and 30% of its cat litter.

The pet product supply chain is unusually decentralised. Ningbo anchors pet beds, carriers, and textile accessories. Guangzhou runs the largest physical wholesale markets. Shenzhen leads smart pet technology — automatic feeders, GPS trackers, pet cameras — while Dongguan's plush toy cluster feeds directly into the pet toy pipeline. For importers, this means no single city covers everything, but the right combination of two or three cities covers most product lines.

Zhejiang’s pet product powerhouse — home to Tianyuan/Petstar and dense clusters for pet beds, carriers, and textile accessories

Ningbo sits at the centre of Zhejiang’s pet product manufacturing belt. Hangzhou Tianyuan Pet Products (listed as 301335.SZ in 2022, trading as Petstar) operates from the greater Ningbo–Hangzhou corridor with 9 factories, 1,600 employees, and 230,000 square metres of production space. Their product range covers pet beds, cat trees, pet apparel, leashes, collars, toys, and electronic accessories — over 30,000 SKUs shipped to 60 countries, serving Walmart, Petco, Chewy, and Fressnapf.

Beyond Tianyuan, Ningbo hosts dozens of mid-sized pet product manufacturers specialising in soft goods: pet beds, cushions, carriers, and textile-based toys. The city’s strength in fabric sourcing (proximity to Shaoxing textiles) and filling materials gives it a cost advantage in anything sewn or stuffed. Ningbo Wellpets, Ningbo Innest, and Ningbo Lewo (with an 800 sqm city-centre showroom) are notable exporters.

For importers, Ningbo is the natural starting point for pet beds, carriers, soft toys, and textile accessories. The port of Ningbo-Zhoushan — the world’s busiest by cargo tonnage — means your goods move from factory to container ship with minimal inland logistics cost. Combine a Ningbo visit with nearby Pingyang (Wenzhou) for chews and leashes to cover most non-food pet product categories in a single trip.

Qingping wholesale market, Canton Fair host, and southern China’s largest pet product trading hub

Guangzhou is China’s pet product trading capital. Qingping Pet Product Wholesale Market, located by the Pearl River in Liwan district, is one of the largest pet product wholesale markets in the country — offering everything from pet toys and grooming tools to beds, bowls, and apparel at competitive wholesale pricing. Huadi Bay Flower, Bird, Fish and Insects Market covers 100,000 square metres and adds aquarium supplies and live pet accessories to the mix.

The biannual Canton Fair (Phase 2, Consumer Goods) and the annual CIPS — China International Pet Show, held at the Canton Fair Complex every November with 1,300+ exhibitors and 80,000 trade visitors from 120 countries — make Guangzhou the single most important city for meeting pet product suppliers face-to-face. CIPS is Asia-Pacific’s largest pet industry exhibition and covers everything from pet food to smart devices.

Guangzhou itself also has a manufacturing base for pet apparel, pet textiles, and flexible packaging, drawing on the Pearl River Delta’s garment and printing supply chains. For a first sourcing trip, Guangzhou gives you the widest exposure to the pet product supply chain in the shortest time.

District 5 of the world’s largest small commodities market — 100+ pet product booths, ultra-low MOQs, side-by-side comparison

Yiwu International Trade Mart’s District 5 houses a dedicated pet products section on the 2nd floor with over 100 booths. Products include pet toys, rope toys, chains, beds, bowls, grooming tools, pet clothing, shoes, scarves, bird cages, and dog crates. Dog accessories are the best-selling category, with dozens of styles for leashes, beds, houses, clothes, and interactive toys.

Yiwu’s advantage is variety and low barriers to entry. Many booths sell from stock with MOQs as low as a few dozen pieces, making it ideal for testing product lines or building mixed-category orders. The market operates 09:00–17:00 every day of the year except a 15-day Spring Festival closure. For importers who want to see and touch hundreds of pet products in a single day, Yiwu is unmatched.

The city also connects to nearby Pingyang (about 3 hours by road) for pet chews and leashes, and to Ningbo (2 hours) for pet beds and textile accessories. A Yiwu–Ningbo–Pingyang circuit covers the bulk of Zhejiang’s pet product supply chain.

China’s smart pet technology hub — automatic feeders, GPS trackers, pet cameras, and IoT devices

Shenzhen dominates the smart pet technology segment. Companies like Petkit (revenue over RMB 1.1 billion in 2024, 90%+ from smart devices), Petrust, OMNI Petlife, and Shenzhen Woof Pet Supplies manufacture automatic feeders, smart water fountains, GPS pet trackers, pet cameras, bark collars, smart retractable leashes, and wireless pet fences. The city’s electronics ecosystem — PCB houses, IoT chip suppliers, injection moulders, and app developers — creates a complete supply chain for any connected pet device.

Dongmen Pet Supplies Street in Luohu district is Shenzhen’s physical wholesale hub, with roughly half its customers coming from Southeast Asia, Europe, Africa, and the Middle East. The market is small in scale compared to Guangzhou or Yiwu but skews heavily toward export-grade and premium products.

For importers sourcing smart pet products, GPS trackers, or any electronic pet device, Shenzhen is the only realistic starting point in China. Factories here have deep experience with FCC, CE, and Bluetooth/Wi-Fi certification for connected devices.

Plush pet toys, rubber chews, and injection-moulded accessories — drawing on the world’s largest toy manufacturing cluster

Dongguan’s toy manufacturing cluster — the largest in the world — feeds directly into the pet toy supply chain. Factories like Aner Beibei Toys (10,000 sqm, 300 workers, Disney/Walmart certified), Plushtoysmfg (250,000 units/month capacity), and Yi Kang Plush Toys (5,000 sqm, est. 2013) produce custom plush pet toys, stuffed animals, rope toys, and rubber chew toys for both domestic and international brands.

The city’s certifications infrastructure is a major advantage — ICTI, Sedex, BSCI, ISO 9001, and ASTM F963 compliance are standard across established factories. Dongguan also has strong injection moulding capabilities for hard pet accessories: feeders, water bowls, pet doors, and crate components.

For importers sourcing plush pet toys, rubber or TPR chews, or any moulded pet accessory, Dongguan is a 30-minute train ride from Guangzhou. Visit both cities on the same trip to cover soft goods, hard goods, and the wholesale markets.

How to source pet products from China

Four ways to find pet product suppliers. Most successful importers use multiple approaches at different stages.

Search on 1688

1688 is Alibaba's domestic B2B marketplace — where Chinese factories trade with each other. For pet products, that means factory-direct pricing on everything from pet beds and chew toys to cat litter and smart feeders, often 20–40% below Alibaba.com prices.

How to find pet product suppliers on 1688

1
Search in Chinese

Use product names in Chinese: 宠物用品 (pet supplies), 猫粮 (cat food), 狗粮 (dog food), 狗玩具 (dog toys), 宠物窝 (pet bed/house), 宠物牵引绳 (pet leash), 猫爪板 (cat scratcher).

2
Filter by pet product hubs

Set the supplier location to Pingyang/Wenzhou (平阳/温州) for chews and leashes, Ningbo (宁波) for pet beds and carriers, Nanhe/Xingtai (南和/邢台) for pet food and cat litter, or Shenzhen (深圳) for smart pet devices.

3
Check factory badges

Look for 实力商家 (verified merchant) and 源头工厂 (source factory). For pet food, check for SC production licence (生产许可证) and any export certifications (FDA registered, BRC, ISO 22000).

4
Verify before paying

Cross-check the business registration and any food safety or certification claims. Pay through 1688 World Pay for CNY payment protection.

How to source pet products from China

Four ways to find pet product suppliers. Most successful importers use multiple approaches at different stages.

Search on 1688

1688 is Alibaba's domestic B2B marketplace — where Chinese factories trade with each other. For pet products, that means factory-direct pricing on everything from pet beds and chew toys to cat litter and smart feeders, often 20–40% below Alibaba.com prices.

How to find pet product suppliers on 1688

1
Search in Chinese

Use product names in Chinese: 宠物用品 (pet supplies), 猫粮 (cat food), 狗粮 (dog food), 狗玩具 (dog toys), 宠物窝 (pet bed/house), 宠物牵引绳 (pet leash), 猫爪板 (cat scratcher).

2
Filter by pet product hubs

Set the supplier location to Pingyang/Wenzhou (平阳/温州) for chews and leashes, Ningbo (宁波) for pet beds and carriers, Nanhe/Xingtai (南和/邢台) for pet food and cat litter, or Shenzhen (深圳) for smart pet devices.

3
Check factory badges

Look for 实力商家 (verified merchant) and 源头工厂 (source factory). For pet food, check for SC production licence (生产许可证) and any export certifications (FDA registered, BRC, ISO 22000).

4
Verify before paying

Cross-check the business registration and any food safety or certification claims. Pay through 1688 World Pay for CNY payment protection.
